What If Humans Have Senses We Haven’t Evolved to Perceive?

Problem

Humans describe reality through five senses, but biological systems suggest perception is a filtered reconstruction, not direct access to reality.

Core Insight

Perception is not reception. It is neural reconstruction from limited sensory input shaped by evolution and prediction systems.

Why It Happens

Sensory systems evolved for survival efficiency, not completeness. The brain prioritizes useful models over full environmental representation.

Lenses

Biology • Neuroscience • Evolution • Philosophy

Main Exploration

Humans usually describe reality through five senses: sight, hearing, smell, taste, and touch. That structure feels complete because it matches how life is experienced from the inside. But biologically, that framework is more like a summary than a full inventory.
